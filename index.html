🌠 Comet ATLAS (C/2024 G3)

Overview

Comet ATLAS (C/2024 G3) is an interstellar comet discovered by the ATLAS survey. This remarkable visitor from beyond our solar system is making its way through our cosmic neighborhood.

  • Discovery: ATLAS (Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System)
  • Type: Long-period comet, potentially interstellar origin
  • Status: Approaching perihelion (closest approach to the Sun)

Unique Characteristics

  • Brightness: Expected to brighten as it approaches the Sun
  • Trajectory: Hyperbolic orbit suggesting possible extrasolar origin
  • Composition: Icy body with potential for spectacular tail development
  • Visibility: May become visible to naked eye at peak brightness
  • Scientific Value: Provides insights into materials from outside our solar system

Observational Data

Orbital Period Unknown (possible parabolic/hyperbolic)
Origin Potentially interstellar
Magnitude Variable (brightening)

🚀 Interplanetary Spacecraft & Probes

Humanity's ambassadors to the stars - spacecraft that have left or are leaving our solar system

🛰️ Voyager 1

Status: Active - Interstellar Space

Launch Date: September 5, 1977

Distance from Earth: ~24 billion km (160+ AU)

Current Status: First human-made object in interstellar space (crossed heliopause in 2012)

  • Explored Jupiter and Saturn
  • Carries Golden Record with sounds and images from Earth
  • Still transmitting data after 45+ years
  • Traveling at ~17 km/s relative to the Sun

🛰️ Voyager 2

Status: Active - Interstellar Space

Launch Date: August 20, 1977

Distance from Earth: ~20 billion km (134+ AU)

Current Status: Second spacecraft in interstellar space (crossed heliopause in 2018)

  • Only spacecraft to visit Uranus and Neptune
  • Grand Tour of outer planets
  • Continues to send scientific data
  • Traveling at ~15.4 km/s relative to the Sun

🛰️ Pioneer 10

Status: Inactive - Beyond Solar System

Launch Date: March 2, 1972

Distance from Earth: ~130+ AU (estimated)

Current Status: Last signal received in 2003, continuing outward journey

  • First spacecraft to traverse asteroid belt
  • First to obtain close-up images of Jupiter
  • Carries Pioneer plaque with human figures and Earth's location
  • Heading toward constellation Taurus

🛰️ Pioneer 11

Status: Inactive - Beyond Solar System

Launch Date: April 5, 1973

Distance from Earth: ~100+ AU (estimated)

Current Status: Last signal received in 1995, continuing outward

  • First spacecraft to study Saturn
  • Also explored Jupiter
  • Discovered Saturn's F ring and new moon
  • Heading toward constellation Aquila

🛰️ New Horizons

Status: Active - Kuiper Belt

Launch Date: January 19, 2006

Distance from Earth: ~58+ AU

Current Status: Exploring Kuiper Belt, will eventually leave solar system

  • First mission to Pluto (July 2015)
  • Explored Kuiper Belt Object Arrokoth (2019)
  • Fastest spacecraft ever launched from Earth
  • On trajectory to eventually enter interstellar space

🛰️ Parker Solar Probe

Status: Active - Inner Solar System

Launch Date: August 12, 2018

Mission: Study the Sun's corona

Current Status: Making close approaches to the Sun

  • Fastest human-made object (430,000+ mph)
  • Closest spacecraft to the Sun
  • Studying solar wind and magnetic fields
  • Will make multiple solar flybys through 2025
